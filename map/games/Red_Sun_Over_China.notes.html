Movement

Movement Through Cities
  • All territories bordering on a city are considered adjacent to each other, but players may only use this indirect connection if they control the city during the start of their turn.

Railroads
  • Railroad lines are marked on the map as dotted lines connecting the railroad stations and two stations are considered connected if there exists a direct dotted line path between them.
  • Trains may move between stations if allies control the two cities which the stations are in and the territories through which the rail lines passes.
  • You must have control of the rail territories at the beginning of your turn in order to use it.

Movement Restrictions
  • Thailand cannot move outside of Thailand, neutral Indochina, and all the original British territories. You can tell which territories belong to the Thailand sphere of movement by looking at the relief tiles--they are the only ones with English words/Roman letters appearing in the background texture.
  • Communists cannot enter Thailand, neutral Indochina, and all the original British territories.
  • Nationalists cannot enter Thailand or Indochina.

Production
  • Communists produce all their units outside of factories, and may also build on newly captured territory. Communists may produce up to 3 units in each territory. Communist have no capital, the flag in the map is merely decoration.

  • Thailand produce all their units outside factories, and may ONLY build inside Thailand (their original starting territories). Thailand may produce up to 4 units in each territory.
  • Nationalists may produce infantry, cavalry and conscripts outside of factories (in the countryside). All other units may be produced in factories only. Nationalists may produce up to 3 units in each non-factory territory.
  • Japanese, Manchukwo, and British follow standard production rules (build all units in factories only, may NOT build outside of factories).
  • Factories in China start out damaged. This is not a bug. All cities in China (except the Nationalist capital Chungking) start out with maximum damage. In order to get the factory operational, players must pay repair price. For all Allied powers, this surcharge is 2 PU. For the Japanese, it is 3 PU. In other words if the Japanese wanted to build more than just 1 unit per Chinese factory per turn, it costs them a premium of 3 PU per additional unit built.

Miscellaneous

Politics
  • The Nationalists and Communists start at war with the Axis powers but the British start as neutral (can't attack or be attacked by Axis powers until war is declared).
  • The Axis powers have the political option to declare war on the British at the start of any of their turns.
  • If by the start of the British turn 4 they are still neutral then they automatically declare war on the Axis powers.

Territory Ownership
  • Any territory recaptured from the Japanese within China belongs to the liberator, not initial owner.

Canals and Map Clarifications
  • The Strait of Malacca: Movement between SZ 17 Malay Sea and SZ 21 Strait of Malacca determined by control of Malaya-Singapore.
  • Pacific-Indian Ocean connection: SZ 22 and SZ 23 is a canal that can ONLY be controlled by the British after Phase II. It takes 2 spaces to move through that canal.
  • Himalayas: Borders marked with X marks indicate that a connection does not exist because of impassable Himalayan mountains.
  • Canton does not touch the ocean.
  • West Kwangtung touches Hong Kong.
